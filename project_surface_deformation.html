Surface Deformation Control of a Ferrofluid-Based Robotic Sheet for Object Handling

Abstract

This paper describes a deformation control of a ferrofluid-based robotic sheet for object handling. The robotic sheet consists of ferrofluid wrapped with thin polyethylene sheets. The robotic sheet allows us to handle objects by controlling its deformation due to the characteristics of a ferrofluid. In order to control its deformation, we need its mechanical model. Although the mechanical model of the ferrofluid has already been researched, the effect of the polyethylene sheet on the ferrofluid has not been considered. This paper verifies the relationship between the fluid shape in the robotic sheet and the thickness of the sheet on the fluid. Additionally, the paper explores transportation methods by making the robotic sheet move two balls simultaneously and a thin circular plate on its surface. The study confirms the deformation control of this robotic sheet in alternative configurations.

Introduction

Ferrofluids are colloidal liquids made of nanoscale ferromagnetic particles suspended in a carrier fluid. They become strongly magnetized in the presence of a magnetic field. This property allows the robotic sheet to manipulate and move liquids and solids effectively.

Robotic Sheet Structure

The robotic sheet consists of ferrofluid enclosed in a polyethylene film, which is highly hydrophobic and oleophobic, making it ideal for manipulating liquid droplets. The sheet deforms in response to magnetic fields generated by electromagnets, enabling precise control over the movement and mixing of liquids.

Experimental Setup and Results

The system utilizes visual feedback control with a camera to monitor the position of the liquid. The electromagnets are adjusted based on the visual data to move the liquid to the desired position. The performance and the effects of different amounts of ferrofluid and numbers of electromagnets were tested in various experiments.

  • Ferrofluid Amount: The amount of ferrofluid significantly affects the sheet's deformation, with an optimal amount required for effective manipulation.
  • Number of Electromagnets: Increasing the number of electromagnets improves control accuracy but reduces the deformation speed.
  • Mixing Characteristics: Higher vibration frequencies (above 15Hz) result in more efficient liquid mixing.
  • Movement Characteristics: Larger liquid volumes increase movement speed but decrease movement precision.

Conclusion and Future Work

This study demonstrates the feasibility of using a ferrofluid-based robotic sheet for automated liquid manipulation. Future research will focus on optimizing the ferrofluid properties and film elasticity to enhance system performance and exploring potential applications in various fields.
